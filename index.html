About ZenHackAdemy

Cybersecurity offers a wide range of attractive career opportunities in a variety of sectors (industry, governmental organizations, and research institutions). Academia is called upon to help students to seize these opportunities. Although many universities are offering courses providing the technical and methodological background necessary to undertake them, most university offerings fall short in providing the practical skills and mindset required by Cybersecurity experts. This type of skills are currently developed by the Ethical Hacking community through the organization of live-fire Cybersecurity (e.g. Capture-the-Flag) competitions.

ZenHackAdemy is a collaborative project between the Computer Security Research Laboratory at DIBRIS of the University of Genoa and the ZenHack ethical hacking team that aims to bridge the gap between these two types of training and to develop new Cybersecurity training techniques and tools to train the next generation of Cybersecurity experts.

To get in touch, please write to prof. Alessandro Armando (Computer Security Laboratory) or to info@zenhack.team.

Projects

Over the years ZenHackAdemy has contributed to the following initiatives:

  • Leonardo-UniGe Cybersecurity Scholarship Project (a.y. 2020-21) by carrying out the hands-on training sessions, by setting up and running the CTF, and by contributing to design and implmentation of the Cyber Defence Simulation
  • Boeing-UniGe Scholarship Project (a.y. 2017-18, a.y. 2018-19, a.y. 2019-20): by designing and carrying out the hands-on training sessions as well as by setting up and running the CTF
  • CyberChallenge.IT (academic years 2017-18, 2018-19, 2019-20, 2020-21): ZenHackAdemy has contributed to training the UniGe Team.
